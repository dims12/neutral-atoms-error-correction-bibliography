# Magne — Denmark's Quantum Computer

**Magne** is a fault-tolerant ("Level 2") quantum computer being built in Denmark, often
mistaken for "Magnum" or "Magna." Its name comes from Norse mythology (Magne, son of Thor),
fitting its Nordic branding.

## Key Specs
- **50 logical qubits**, built from ~**1,225 physical** neutral-atom qubits
- Billed as the world's first commercially available **Level 2** (logical-qubit,
  error-corrected) quantum computer

## Who's Behind It
- **Hardware:** Atom Computing (neutral-atom technology)
- **Software / orchestration:** Microsoft (Azure-based control stack, error correction, AI agents)
- **Owner / operator:** QuNorth, a new Nordic quantum venture
- **Funding:** €80 million from EIFO (Denmark's export & investment fund) and the
  Novo Nordisk Foundation

## Details
- **Location:** Innovation District Copenhagen, Denmark
- **Timeline:** Expected operational by **end of 2026**

## Physical Platform
Magne runs on a **neutral-atom platform**:
- **Qubits = individual neutral atoms** — Atom Computing uses **ytterbium (Yb-171)** atoms.
- Atoms are trapped and arranged with **optical tweezers** (tightly focused laser beams)
  forming an array.
- Qubit states are encoded in stable **nuclear-spin states**; lasers drive gates and
  entanglement via **Rydberg interactions**.

**Why neutral atoms:**
- **Identical qubits** — every atom is exactly the same (no fabrication variation).
- **Scalability** — optical arrays can trap 1,000+ atoms.
- **Long coherence times** — nuclear-spin qubits hold their state well, aiding error correction.

**Compared to other platforms:**
- **Superconducting** (IBM, Google) — fabricated chips run near absolute zero.
- **Trapped ions** (IonQ, Quantinuum) — charged atoms held by electromagnetic fields.
- **Neutral atoms** (Atom Computing, Pasqal, QuEra) — Magne's platform.

## Why It Matters
Logical qubits combine many error-prone physical qubits into one stable, error-corrected
qubit — making Magne far more capable than machines measured by raw physical-qubit count alone.

> Note: An unrelated EU project, **PROMETHEUS**, separately targets a 1,000-qubit machine
> to restore Europe's lead in quantum computing.
